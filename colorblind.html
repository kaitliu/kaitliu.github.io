Overview

This project focuses on improving accessibility for colorblind individuals in digital design environments. Our goal was to create a tool that helps users more accurately identify/differentiate colors, shade identification, and choosing color palettes that are cohesive and aesthetically pleasing. Through research and ideation, our team developed a web extension prototype that support those who are colorblind in design spaces.

Insights

  1. Many everyday tasks depend on color without alternate options such as matching clothing or determining food readiness.
  2. Those with colorblindness are typically ignored or overlooked by people since their credibility is lowered due to their colorblindness.
  3. There is a lack of accessible and convenient services that assist with colorblindness.

Co-Design Session

"How might we help colorblind individuals accurately identify and differentiate colors in digital spaces?"

Key Challenges Identified:

  • While designs may look good to them, they often can't confidently identify the colors they are using.
  • Since they can't always recognize the colors used in a design, giving feedback on color choices is difficult.

Design Principles

Our design should...

  • Prioritize clarity and ease of use through visual cues and alternative indicators that are not color-dependent to ensure individuals with colorblindness can navigate through without frustration
  • Fit into existing workflows and environments rather than requiring users to adapt to new systems or tools
  • Help individuals with colorblindness easily identify and select colors in digital environments, such as design platforms like Canva where color differentiation is crucial
  • Provide immediate solutions for individuals with colorblindness to overcome challenges with color selection and differentiation in digital spaces
